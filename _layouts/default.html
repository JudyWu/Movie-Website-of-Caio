Caio Ribeiro

Caio Ribeiro is a native of Brazil. Caio's first short film, Ballad for Two Lovers, won the best director's award at the Brussels Film Festival in Belgium in 1999.
Caio's first feature film, Sometime in August, was named an official selection at the Los Angeles Independent Film Festival, the Florida Independent Film Festival, and nominated for the best American independent film of 1999.

From 1999 to 2004, Caio wrote and served as director of photography for two World War II films, Last Letters from Monte Rosa and
The Fallen.
Both films were winners of multiple awards and distributed internationally. In 2007 Caio started working on The Realm, a series that pays homage to The Twilight Zone.

Caio is currently working on a three part project:

I - A full length documentary about Jorge Luis Borges' story "The Intruder", and its hidden symbols, that reveals a Borges unknown to the general public.

II - An Interactive Installation
where players create alternate versions of "The Intruder", from the point of view of Juliana Burgos, a character Borges named after himself, but who he refuses to give a voice in the story.

III - A full length ballet of "The Intruder" that will premiere in the National Library of Buenos Aires, and subsequently travel to libraries around the world.
