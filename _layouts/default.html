Introduction to Caio, the filmmaker

Caio Ribeiro is a native of Brazil. Caio's first short film, "Ballad for Two Lovers", won the best director's award at the Huy Film Festival in Belgium in YEAR. Caio's first feature film, "Sometime in August", was named an official selection at the Los Angeles Independent Film Festival, the Florida Independent Film Festival, and nominated for the Starfish Award at the Hamptons Film Festival.
From 1999 to 2004, Caio wrote and served as director of photography for two World War II films, "Letters from the Dead" and "The Fallen". Both films were winners of multiple awards and distributed internationally. In 2007 Caio started working on "The Realm", a series that pays homage to The Twilight Zone.

Sometime In August

A lonely woman hires a private investigator to find out who she really is.

Marcia has just been dumped once again. Lonely and desperate, she hires a private investigator to follow and evaluate her in the hopes that can help her recreate herself into someone a man can love. The P.I. watches as a desperate Marcia tries to reinvent herself into a new woman who tries to impress him at any cost and, in the process, starts to fall in love with the real Marcia she is so determined to hide from him.

Last Letters From Monte Rosa

Life through the eyes of doomed soldiers late in WWII.

Northern Italy, late 1944. A German Army lieutenant finds that his Italian reinforcements are in worse shape than the Germans they are supposed to support, leading to conflict with his Italian counterpart. Harassed by Allied bombardment and local guerilla forcs, the German and Italian lieutenants need to find a way to work things out... before the enemy does it for them.

Ballad For Two Lovers

A man contemplates about love, life and death while photographing his latest lover, who he's sure is about to give him the boot.

Frank is sure Juliette is about to give him the boot. He decides to photograph every inch of her body and keep a file on her for posterity, as he has done with his previous lovers. Juliette, in turn, can't resist toying with his fears, as well as pondering on whether to really leave him or not.

The Realm Series
